In the **Noise Processing** stage (also known as Noise Injection), the [[Media Manager (MM)]] applies specific environmental audio layers to the clean recordings to simulate real-world AI use cases. Following the project-specific guidelines, the MM mixes the clean speech with designated noise profiles—such as "Street Ambience," "Office Chatter," or "Cafe Background"—at precise Signal-to-Noise Ratios (SNR). This process is vital for stress-testing speech recognition models, transforming the high-quality studio-standard audio into a diverse dataset of "dirty" audio that mimics spontaneous public conversation.
