Once the raw recording is submitted, it enters the **Initial Processing** stage, handled by the [[ Media Manager (MM)]]. This technical phase focuses on preparing the "clean" audio for downstream tasks by performing non-destructive edits, such as DC offset removal, loudness normalization to a target LUFS level, and channel mapping (ensuring Speaker 1 and Speaker 2 are correctly isolated on Left/Right or separate mono tracks). The goal is to standardize the varied recording environments of different speakers into a uniform technical format before any linguistic analysis or artificial degradation occurs.
